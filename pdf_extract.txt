SECTION 1
How to Use the Updated Payroll
Process
Running Payroll — Step by Step
1Run Payroll for Active Staff
Initiate payroll as you normally would through the HR payroll section. The
system will automatically generate payroll for all currently active employees
for the current month.
No changes to the way you start a payroll run — the improvements happen
automatically in the background.
2Confirm the Payroll Period
The system will now correctly calculate from the first to the last day of the
month. You do not need to manually adjust or verify the month-end date —
this is handled automatically.
3Review the Deduction Breakdown on Each Payslip
When reviewing generated payslips, you will now see a clearly itemised
deduction breakdown. Confirm that each of the following lines is present and
makes sense for the staff member:

4Verify the First Payroll Run After This Update
For the first payroll cycle after this update, take extra care to spot-check a
few payslips before releasing them to staff. Compare the figures to your
expected deduction values and confirm the CRA appears as its own separate
line item.
A sample of 3 to 5 payslips from different salary bands is usually sufficient
for a confidence check.
5Keep Tax Settings Up to Date
The payroll engine uses centrally configured tax, pension, and NHF rates. If
government policy changes — such as a revised pension rate or a new tax
threshold — update these central settings and all future payroll runs will
automatically use the new rates. No manual adjustments per staff member
are required.

SECTION 2
How to Use Auto-Graded
Performance Goals
Previously, HR staff had to manually review and score each
completed employee goal — a time-consuming process that
could also introduce inconsistency between reviewers. The
system now automatically grades goals  once they are
marked complete, based on the criteria set by your team.
HR's role shifts from manual scoring to reviewing and
validating the results.
Managing Performance Goals — Step by Step
1Set Up or Review Staff Goals
Create or update staff goals using the existing HR goals workflow. For auto-
grading to work accurately, each goal must have clearly defined success
criteria. The more specific the criteria, the more reliable the automatic grade
will be.
Goals with vague or missing criteria will still require manual review. Use this
update as an opportunity to tighten up goal definitions going forward.
2Let the System Grade Completed Goals
Once a goal is marked as complete — either by the employee or their line
manager — the system will evaluate it automatically against the configured
criteria and assign a grade. No further action is needed for standard
objectives.

=== PAGE 7 ===
3Review the Auto-Grade Results
HR and line managers can view the automatically assigned grades on the
goal performance dashboard or individual goal records. These results can be
used directly to inform performance conversations, appraisal scores, or
incentive decisions.
Auto-grades are not final by default — HR or a manager can still override a
grade where context requires human judgement.
4Adjust Grading Criteria When Business Priorities Change
If organisational goals shift — such as a new performance framework or
revised KPIs — update the goal scoring criteria within the system. All goals
created or completed after the update will be graded using the new criteria
automatically.
✅ Quick Checklist for This Performance Cycle
Ensure all active staff goals have clearly defined success criteria set.
Review auto-grade results on the goal dashboard at the end of the cycle.
Flag any auto-graded results that need human review or override.
Update grading criteria if business performance priorities have changed.
Use auto-grade results to support appraisal and incentive discussions.

SECTION 3
Practical Recommendations & Next
Steps
Recommended Actions for Management
🔍
Run a validation payroll cycle immediately after the update.
Before releasing payslips to staff, verify a small sample of payroll
outputs to confirm that figures are correct. Comparing 3–5 payslips
across different salary bands will give confidence that the update is
working as expected.
📋
Check that CRA appears as a separate deduction line.  One of the
visible changes on payslips is a dedicated CRA line. If this is absent on
any payslip, flag it for review before staff are paid.
⚙️
Keep the central tax and pension settings maintained.  The
accuracy of payroll going forward depends on these settings being
current. Whenever a government policy changes — pension rates, PAYE
thresholds, or NHF rates — update the central configuration promptly.
This is the only change HR needs to make; individual payslips update
automatically.
🎯
Review goal auto-grade results in the next performance cycle.
This is the first cycle with automatic grading enabled. HR should review
the generated grades and validate that they align with expectations
before using them for formal appraisals.
📝
Tighten goal success criteria for all active objectives.  Auto-
grading is most reliable when criteria are clearly defined. Use the start
of this cycle to review and sharpen any goals with vague or missing
success metrics.
