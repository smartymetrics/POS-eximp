Chapter 2

Campaigns

A Campaign is a single marketing email that you design, write, and send to a group of people. Think of it like printing and delivering custom flyers — except you can track exactly who read them and who threw them away.

Campaign Statuses Explained

✏️

Draft

You're still working on it. Only you can see it. Nothing has been sent yet.

🕐

Scheduled

You've approved it and set a specific time for it to go out automatically.

🚀

Sending

Emails are being delivered to recipients right now.

✅

Sent

All emails delivered. You can now view the full analytics report.

Step-by-Step: Creating Your First Campaign

  • 1
    Click "New Campaign"

    A small form will appear. Give your campaign a clear name like "April Property Launch — Lekki" and write the email subject line (e.g. "Yours could be the best address in Lagos 🏡").

  • 2
    Open the Editor

    Click "Edit Draft" to open the full email designer. This is where you design the actual email your leads will receive. (See Chapter 3 for the full Editor guide.)

  • 3
    Click "Send Campaign"

    Once your design is ready, click the green Send button. You'll see two options: "Send Now" (instant! Goes out within minutes) or "Schedule for Later" (pick a future date & time).

  • 4
    Choose Your Audience

    You can send to "All Contacts", any custom segment you've created, or paste in a specific list of emails manually.

  • 5
    View the Report

    After sending, click the 👁️ "View Report" button on the campaign to see who opened it, who clicked, and who bounced.

Scheduling — Your Secret Weapon

The best time to send marketing emails in Nigeria is Tuesday or Wednesday, between 9 AM and 11 AM. People are alert at work and checking their inbox. Avoid Monday mornings (too busy) and Friday afternoons (everyone's winding down). Use the scheduler to hit this window perfectly — even if you're on a site visit at the time.

Duplicating a Campaign

If you have a campaign that performed really well, don't start from scratch. Use the Duplicate button (the copy icon). It makes an exact copy as a new Draft, which you can then tweak and send to a different audience. This is great for A/B testing.

✅ Quick Win: After a campaign is sent, use the Report's "Recipients" tab to see which contacts engaged. These are the people you should follow up with by phone — they've already shown interest.

Viewing a Contact's Full Profile

Click the ⋮ menu next to any contact and select "View Profile & History." You'll see a full interaction timeline — every campaign they received, every email they opened, every link they clicked — in chronological order. This is gold for your sales team before they make a call.

Chapter 5

Segments — Laser-Focused Targeting

A Segment is a smart, filtered group of contacts. Instead of sending the same email to everyone and hoping it's relevant, you send targeted messages to specific groups who are most likely to respond. This is how the big players do it.

🧠 Think of it this way: Imagine you're a doctor. You wouldn't give every patient the same medicine. A Segment helps you give each group exactly what they need. Overdue clients need a payment reminder. New leads need an introduction. Hot prospects need an irresistible offer.

The Built-In Smart Segments (Ready to Use)

These are pre-built and always up-to-date automatically — no setup needed:

✨

Engaged Audience

Everyone who has opened at least one email. These people know who you are — great for follow-up campaigns.

🔥

Hot Leads

Everyone with an Engagement Score above 50. These are people who keep interacting with you. Prioritize these!

🆕

Recent Signups

Anyone who joined your list in the last 30 days. Perfect for a "Welcome to the Eximp & Cloves family" campaign.

🔴

Overdue Clients

Clients whose invoice due date has passed and still have a balance. Automatically calculated from your Finance system.

🟡

Outstanding Balance

Clients who have made partial payments but still owe money. Great for a gentle reminder campaign.

🟢

Fully Paid

Clients who have completed all payments. A great group to celebrate, ask for referrals, or upsell new phases.

Creating Your Own Custom Segments

Click "Build Custom Segment" to create your own group using rules. You can combine multiple rules for very precise targeting.

Available Rules:

  • Engagement Score is greater than / less than [number]
  • Lead Type is equal to "Lead" or "Client"
  • Lead Source contains "Lagos Expo" (target people from a specific event)
  • Last Opened is in last [X] days (e.g. active in last 7 days)
  • Has opened campaign... — Did they open a specific campaign?
  • Has clicked link in... — Did they click a link in a specific campaign?

How to Use a Segment

Click any segment card and it immediately filters your Contacts list to show only those people. You can then send them a campaign by clicking "Send Campaign" and choosing that segment as your audience. The system counts these people in real-time, so you always know exactly how many people you're reaching.

🏆 Best Practice Playbook:
Week 1: Send a "New Property Launch" email to ALL contacts.
Week 2: Resend a different version to the segment "Opened but didn't click" from Week 1.
Week 3: Call or WhatsApp everyone in the "Hot Leads" segment personally.
This three-step flow consistently outperforms blasting everyone with the same message repeatedly.

Chapter 6

Sequences (Auto) — Your 24/7 Sales Machine

A Sequence is a series of emails that get sent to someone automatically over a period of days or weeks. You set it up once, and then it runs by itself — forever — for every new lead or client that enters it. This is the most powerful feature in the system.

💡 Real-World Example: A new lead submits their details on your website. At that exact moment, the system automatically enrolls them in a "New Lead Welcome Sequence." Day 0: They get a warm welcome email. Day 3: They get an email showcasing your top properties. Day 7: They get a "Limited Units Available" urgency email. Day 14: They get a testimonial email from a happy client. All of this happens without any human involvement.

How a Sequence Works — Step by Step

  • 1
    You create the sequence and its steps

    Give it a name ("New Lead Welcome"), set a trigger, and add as many email steps (drips) as you want, each with a delay in days.

  • 2
    Someone gets enrolled

    Either you add them manually, or the system adds them automatically based on the trigger (e.g. a new client is registered).

  • 3
    The engine runs hourly

    Every single hour, the system checks: "Are there any emails due to be sent today?" If yes, it sends them automatically.

  • 4
    The sequence completes or exits

    When all steps are done, the contact is marked "Completed." If they become a client mid-sequence, they are automatically "Exited" (so they don't get embarrassing lead-nurturing emails after buying!).

Automation Triggers — How People Enter a Sequence

✋

Manual Enrollment Only

You decide who goes in. You manually add people from the Contacts tab. Good for focused campaigns.

🤖

New Client Registered

The moment a new client is added to the Finance system, they are automatically enrolled. No manual work needed ever.

Smart Branching — The "Intelligent" Part

When creating a sequence with multiple steps, you can make any step (Step 2 and beyond) "Smart" by checking the "Only if Opened Pre-Step" checkbox. This means:

  • If the person opened your previous email → they receive the next step as normal.
  • If they ignored your previous email → the system skips this step rather than spamming them.

This makes your automation behave like a human would — not pestering people who aren't engaging, and rewarding those who are with more content.

Reading the Analytics on a Sequence Card

Each sequence card on the dashboard shows three numbers:

  • Active — People currently working through the sequence right now.
  • Finished — People who completed every step. Congratulations to them!
  • Exited — People who were automatically removed (e.g. they became a client).
📌 Pro Tip: A high "Exited" count on a lead nurturing sequence is actually a great sign! It means lots of leads are converting to clients and being automatically removed from the sequence. That's the system working perfectly.

Chapter 8

Compliance & The Unsubscribe System

This chapter is critical. Sending emails to people who don't want them is not just annoying — it is illegal under international anti-spam laws (GDPR, CAN-SPAM) and can get your entire email domain blacklisted, meaning your emails stop working for everyone. Our system is built to protect you and your brand.

How the Unsubscribe System Works

  • 1
    The Link is Automatic

    Every single email sent from this system contains an unsubscribe link at the footer. It is generated and injected automatically — you do not need to add it yourself.

  • 2
    Branded Landing Page

    When someone clicks "Unsubscribe", they are taken to a professional Eximp & Cloves branded page — not a generic plain page — where they can confirm their unsubscription.

  • 3
    Instant & Permanent

    The moment they confirm, their is_subscribed status is set to false in the database. No more emails will ever be sent to them, even if you accidentally try to include them.

  • 4
    The Hard Block

    The email sending engine checks every recipient before dispatching. If a contact has is_subscribed = false, the email is silently blocked — it will never leave the system.

What You'll See on a Contact's Record

In the Contacts tab, each person has a small colored circle indicating their subscription status:

  • 🟢 Green dot — Active Subscriber (can receive emails)
  • 🔴 Red dot — Unsubscribed (will NOT receive any emails — ever)
⚠️ Important Rule: You must never manually override an unsubscribe. If someone has chosen to opt out, that decision must be respected. Attempting to re-subscribe them or add them to a new list without their explicit consent is a legal violation.

Chapter 10

Smart Drip & Advanced Automation

This chapter covers the "Pro" features of the Smart Drip engine. These tools allow you to create low-touch marketing funnels that respond to lead behavior and segment status automatically.

Behavioral Branching (Selective Drips)

Standard sequences send emails on a fixed schedule. Behavioral Branching allows you to make your sequences "smarter" by conditioning steps on lead engagement:

  • Conditional Sends: You can set a step to "Only send if the contact OPENED the previous email."
  • The "Skip" Logic: If a lead didn't engage with the previous step, the engine will automatically skip them to the next scheduled step instead of sending them irrelevant content.
  • Why use this? It keeps your "Hot Leads" engaged while protecting your email reputation with cold leads.

Segment-Driven Triggers

The manual enrollment button (⚡) is great for individual leads, but Segment Triggers allow for massive automation based on your targeted groups:

  • Zero-Touch Onboarding: Choose "Member of Segment" as your Sequence Trigger.
  • How it works: Every hour, the engine scans your chosen segment. If it finds a contact who isn't in the sequence yet, it enrolls them immediately.
  • Perfect for: "Welcome series" for different lead sources or "Follow-up" series for clients with outstanding balances.
💡 Pro Strategy: Combine these! Create a sequence triggered by your "High Engagement" segment, but use behavioral branching to skip people who stop opening. This ensures your most automated workflows are always your most relevant ones.
